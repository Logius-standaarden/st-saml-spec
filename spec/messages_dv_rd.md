## DV - SAML Message specification

### DV&rarr;RD - SAML authentication proces {#dv-saml-authentication}

SAML authentication messages between [=DV=] (or [=LC=]) and [=RD=] are described in this chapter. First the authentication overview and the steps, then the specification of the individual SAML messages between [=DV=] and [=RD=].

#### DV&rarr;RD - authentication - steps {#dv-saml-authentication-diagram}

Before the above authentication steps are explained, it is important to make a distinction between the [=Front channel=] and the [=Back channel=].
The [=Front channel=] is the communication between the [=DV=] or [=LC=] and [=RD=] via the [=UA=] of the [=EU=] (step 2 & 3 and step 8 & 9 in the figure above). The [=Back channel=] is the direct communication between the [=DV=] or [=LC=] and [=RD=] (step 10 and Step 11 in the figure above). This distinction is important because possibly sensitive data (such as a citizen service number) is never send via the [=Front channel=], so that they can never be intercepted or changed by the [=UA=]. 

**Step 1**

The [=EU=] with a [=UA=] wants to access a part on the web service of the [=DV=] that requires authentication of the [=EU=].

**Step 2 and 3**

The [=DV=] or [=LC=] will send the [=EU=] to [=RD=] for authentication and or proof of representation.

**Step 4 up to and including 7 (out of scope for this flow)**

In this step the [=RD=] will offer the [=EU=] a choice of [=AD=]'s and [=BVD=]'s that fulfil the authentication requirements (e.g. minimum level of assurance required, type of mandate). Optionally, the [=DV=] can pass pre-selection information concerning the [=AD=] to query, and if representation will be used, to the [=RD=].

If the [=EU=] hasn't already chosen in step 2, the [=EU=] chooses an authentication method that meets the minimum required [=LoA=], and also chooses whether to use representation. The [=EU=] is presented with the corresponding [=AD=] login screen. The [=EU=] authenticates with the chosen means.

Optionally, the [=EU=] also chooses a representation method. The [=EU=] is presented with the corresponding screen with choice of who to represent. The [=EU=] chooses the correct [=Represented Party=] to represent.

**Step 8 and 9**

[=RD=] sends the [=EU=] back to the [=DV=] or [=LC=] via a redirect. An opaque [=Artifact=] generated by [=RD=] is send here. This [=Artifact=] refers to the actual [Artifact Response message](#dv-artifact-response) that is waiting at [=RD=]. 

Even if the authentication starting in step 4 was unsuccessful or interrupted, an artifact is send to the [=DV=] or LC. This [=Artifact=] provides an error message with the [Artifact Resolve request](#dv-artifact-resolve) and cannot be exchanged for a valid [Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion).

**Step 10**

With the [=Artifact=] the [=DV=]/[=LC=] sends an [Artifact Resolve request](#dv-artifact-resolve) to the [=RD=] for the result of the (successful or unsuccessful) authentication and (optionally) representation via a [=Back channel=].

Artifacts are stored by [=RD=] for a maximum of 15 minutes and can only be retrieved once by the [=DV=]/[=LC=]. Situations are possible (process breakdown, early logout) where [=RD=] saves an [=Artifact=] for less than 15 minutes.

**Step 11**

[=RD=] replies with an [Artifact Response message](#dv-artifact-response) that belongs to the [=Artifact=]. In this message, [=RD=] provides an [Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) that includes the authentication result and optionally the representation selection. And, if the authentication / representation selection was successful, the requested (encrypted) identifier(s) and attribute(s) of the [=EU=] and optionally the [=Represented Party=]. The identities and attributes are encrypted so that only the intended [=DV=](s) can obtain the plain text value.

If the authentication or attempt to select representation was unsuccessful, it is indicated in the Status Code of the Response message and if possible, a reason is given so that the web service can inform the [=EU=].

**Step 12 & 13**

Successful authentication / selection of representation gives the [=DV=] information needed for access control decision typically resulting in [=EU=] access to service in step 13.

### DV&rarr;RD - SAML messages {#dv-saml-messages} 

The SAML messages are specified in detail in the following sections. The following rules apply

> 1. The messages contain at least the elements that are specified as mandatory by the standard.
> 1. In addition, the messages also contain optional elements. It is indicated whether these elements are mandatory, conditional or optional. With optional elements, it is up to the participants to determine whether they are used.
> 1. Optional SAML elements that are not in this specification SHOULD NOT be included. When present they will be ignored when possible.

#### DV&rarr;RD AuthnRequest {#dv-authnrequest} 

When a principal (or an agent acting on the principal's behalf) wishes to obtain assertions containing authentication statements to establish a security context at one or more relying parties, it can use the authentication request protocol to send an [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message) message element to a SAML authority and request that it returns a [AuthN-Response message](#dv-authn-response-message) containing one or more such assertions.

###### The use of [=dv-authn-request-message/AttributeConsumingServiceIndex=] or [=dv-authn-request-message/Extensions=] for referring to service definitions {#dv-attributeconsumingserviceindex-extensions}

This specification uses the concept of a [=Service Catalog=] kept by [=RD=], which contains amongst others `Service Definitions`. In the [=Service Catalog=] each '<dfn title="has a unique ServiceUUID in the Service Catalog.">Service Definition</dfn>' has a unique `[=ServiceUUID=]`. Each [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message) must refer to a [=Service Definition=] using its `[=ServiceUUID=]`.

An [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message) has two options for this `[=ServiceUUID=]` reference: 
1. Only the [=DV=] MAY provide a `@[=dv-authn-request-message/AttributeConsumingServiceIndex=]` that MUST correspond to an `&#64;[=dv-metadata/Index=]` attribute of an `[=dv-metadata/AttributeConsumingService=]` entry in the [DV Metadata](#dv-metadata). And this `AttributeConsumingService` entry MUST contain a **`[=ServiceUUID=]`**.  When using the `[=dv-authn-request-message/AttributeConsumingServiceIndex=]` the [=RD=] will obtain the [=DV=]'s [=EntityID=] from the `[=dv-authn-request-message/Issuer=]` element in the [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message).
2. Alternatively a [=DV=] MAY use the `[=dv-authn-request-message/Extensions=]` element in the [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message). All other [=Participant=]s MUST use the `[=dv-authn-request-message/Extensions=]` element in the [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message). The `[=dv-authn-request-message/Extensions=]` element contains both the `[=EntityID=]` of the [=DV=] and the explicit **`[=ServiceUUID=]`**.

##### DV&rarr;RD AuthnRequest Processing rules {#dv-authnrequest-processing-rules}
In addition to the processing rules required by the SAML specification the following rule applies. If any of these validations fails, the authentication MUST fail:

1.	[=RD=] MUST validate that the [=DV=] is registered for the requested [=ServiceUUID=] referenced by the index in the [DV metadata](#dv-metadata) or in the [=dv-authn-request-message/Extensions|Extensions element of the DV AuthN-request=] and validate that the registration is valid;

2.	IF an [=LC=] is involved THEN [=RD=] MUST validate that the [=DV=] is registered to use the requested [=ServiceUUID=] with the [=LC=] that sends the [DV AuthN-Request message](#dv-authn-request-message) on behalf of the [=DV=] and that the registration is valid;


#### DV&larr;RD Artifact Binding {#dv-artifact-binding}

The SAML response of a [=RD=] to a [SAML request](#dv-authn-request-message) of a [=DV=] (or [=LC=]) uses an [=Artifact=]-Binding for extra security see  [[SAML2.BINDINGS]] section 3.6.6. Therefor SAML Response does not return the actual SAML message with an Authentication Assertion but an [=Artifact=] as a reference to actual the SAML [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) message. The Artifact is used by the [=DV=] (or [=LC=]) to retrieve the SAML [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) at the [=RD=].

##### DV&larr;RD - Artifact Binding - Message {#dv-saml-response-artifact-binding-message} 
The [=RD=] sends an [=Artifact=] via the [=Front channel=] to the [=DV=]/[=LC=], using a HTTP-redirect of the [=UA|webbrowser=] to the [=dv-metadata/AssertionConsumerService=] referenced in the [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message). 
> e.g. `https://dv.nl/SAML/ACS?SAMLart=AAQAAMh0dHA6Ly9pZHAuZXhhbXBsZS5jb20vU0FNTC9N&RelayState=xyz123`

The [=Artifact=] is only a reference to the SAML [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion). And even if no authentication has taken place, an [=Artifact=] will be send.

###### Processing Rules for Status {#dv-artifactresponse-status}

Special processing rules for [=dv-artifact-response-message/Status=] are described in [[SAML2.BINDINGS]] §3.6.6.

Even if the ArtifactResponse's [=dv-artifact-response-message/Status=] indicates success it may still not contain a [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) conform [[SAML2.BINDINGS]] §3.6.6: 
 
If the [=RD=] receives an [Artifact Resolve message](#dv-artifact-resolve-message) message that it can understand, it MUST return a [AuthN Response message](#dv-artifact-response) with a [=dv-artifact-response-message/StatusCode=] value of urn:oasis:names:tc:SAML:2.0:status:Success`, even if it does not return the corresponding message (for example because the artifact requester is not authorized to receive the message or the artifact is no longer valid).

###### Audience restriction - DV {#dv-audience-restriction}
The [=dv-authn-response-assertion/AudienceRestriction=] element in the [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) must be checked in terms of content. An [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) may only be processed if the [=dv-authn-response-assertion/AudienceRestriction=] contains the [=EntityID=] of the recipient.

> The values for DVs included in [=dv-authn-response-assertion/Audience=] are reflected in the [=dv-subjectconfirmation/Recipient=] attribute of the [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedID=] element in the [Attributestatement](#dv-attributestatement). This does not apply to the [=LC=] or [=RD=] that is only included in the [=dv-authn-response-assertion/AudienceRestriction=]. The [=LC=] or [=RD=] is not, after all, an entity which may receive identities.

###### Level of assurance validation
The [=dv-authn-response-assertion/AuthnContextClassRef=] in the [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) always states the authentication level at which the citizen has authenticated himself. [=DV=]'s MUST be prepared for the [=dv-authn-response-assertion/AuthnContextClassRef=] to contain a higher [=LoA=] than the requested [=LoA=]. [=DV=]'s MUST accept authentications with a level equal to or higher than the minimum level registered for the [=Service=] in the [=Service Catalog=]. [=DV=] must configure minimum [=LoA=] for the [=Service=]  when providing information in the onboarding process. The [=AD=] is responsible for providing the correct [=LoA=] for a given authentication request (equal to or higher than the [=LoA=] registered for the [=Service=] in the [=Service Catalog=])

###### SubjectConfirmation - DV  {#dv-subject-confirmation}
The  [=dv-subjectconfirmation/SubjectConfirmation=]  exists in a [=dv-authn-response-assertion/Subject=] and is used to hold a bearer confirmation in a [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) to an [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message), to conform to the WebSSO profile.
In case a relying party is requesting authentication of an [=EU=] according to the SAML Web SSO profile, a bearer [=dv-subjectconfirmation/SubjectConfirmation=] (see [[SAML2.PROFILES]], §3.3 and §4.1.4) must be provided.
The [=dv-subjectconfirmation/NotOnOrAfter=] in the [=dv-subjectconfirmation/SubjectConfirmationData=] element limits the time during which the [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) result MAY be used to establish an authenticated session for the [=EU=] using the [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion). The [=dv-authn-response-assertion/NotOnOrAfter=] is initially set to +2 minutes relative to the time of issuance of the enclosing [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion). These values can be changed however in time. The `dv-authn-response-assertion/NotBefore` attribute MUST NOT be used.

###### NameID - after decrypting EncryptedID {#nameid}
An [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedID=] MUST contain a SAML `NameID` after decryption, with the following properties:

- The `@Format` attribute MUST be set to `urn:oasis:names:tc:SAML:2.0:nameid-format:persistent`.
- The `@NameQualifier` attribute MUST be populated with the full name of the type of identifying attribute (see [Attribute Identifier Types](#attribute-identifier-types) for the `@NameQualifier` types). 
- The attributes `@SPNameQualifier` and `@SPProvidedID` MUST NOT be used.
- In case more than one certificate is listed for encryption for the recipient in the metadata, the content-encryption-key MUST be encrypted for each certificate. This will result in multiple [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedKey=] each with the same [=dv-attributestatement/Recipient=].

See also: [Example NameID (after decryption)](#example-nameid)

###### Multiple recipients

SAML and XML-encryption allow for multiple recipients of the same encrypted element. The construct for this is specified in more detail in errata E43 of [[SAML2.ERRATA.05]]. In case of multiple recipients:

- each [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedKey=] MUST have a `CarriedKeyName` equal to the `KeyName` used in the `KeyInfo` of the [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedData=].
- each [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedKey=] SHOULD have a `ReferenceList` referring back to the data encrypted with the symmetric key contained.

Upon decryption, elements without an [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedKey=] intended for the decrypting recipient MAY be ignored and [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedKey=] for other recipients of encrypted elements SHOULD be ignored.

>> Rationale: `@ID` values must uniquely identify the elements which bear them. Identifiers that appear in the summary assertion and also in the advice assertion(s) will break XML validation.

> If the type of identity is `urn:nl-eid-gdi:1.0:id:legacy-BSN`, only one [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedData=] element MUST be used in combination with one or more [=dv-attributestatement/EncryptedKey=] elements (one for each recipient).

##### DV&larr;RD AuthN Response - Message processing rules for DV {#dv-authn-response-processing}

Regardless of the SAML binding used, the service provider MUST do the following:

1.	Verify any signatures present on the [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) or the [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion).
1.	Verify that the **dv-subjectconfirmation/@Recipient** attribute in any bearer 
[Subject Confirmationdata](#dfn-subjectconfirmationdata) matches the assertion consumer service URL to which the [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion) or [Artifact](#dv-artifact-response) was delivered.
1.	Verify that the [@NotOnOrAfter](#dv-authn-response-assertion) attribute in any bearer 
[Subject Confirmationdata](#dfn-subjectconfirmationdata) has not passed, subject to allowable clock skew between the providers.
1.	Verify that the &#64;[=dv-subjectconfirmation/InResponseTo=] attribute in the bearer 
[Subject Confirmationdata](#dfn-subjectconfirmationdata) equals the [@ID](#dv-authn-request-message) of its original [AuthN Request message](#dv-authn-request-message) message.
1.	Verify that it's [=EntityID=] is included as [=dv-authn-response-assertion/Audience=] in the [AuthN Response - Assertion](#dv-authn-response-assertion).
1.	Verify that any assertions relied upon are valid in other respects.
7.	Any assertion which is not valid, or whose subject confirmation requirements cannot be met SHOULD be discarded and SHOULD NOT be used to establish a security context for the principal.
